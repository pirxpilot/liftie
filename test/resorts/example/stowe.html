News & Events

We currently have partly to mostly cloudy skies over the mountain, and we have picked up a dusting of new snow overnight. Todayâ€™s forecast calls for occasional snow showers & flurries, with high temperatures in the mid 20s at the base and mid teens at the summit. Thanks to over six feet of snowfall since mid-December at our summit, the skiing & riding continues to be phenomenal. For today, we have 109 trails & 4 terrain parks open, serviced by 11 lifts, with skiing and riding for all ability levels on 40 miles of terrain.

Last night we groomed 53 trails, including Nosedive. We also groomed our 4 open terrain parks, which have a combined total of 28 freestyle terrain features. Please note that the Lower Standard trail & terrain park are closed while we build our half-pipe. Also, Slalom Hill will be reserved today for the FCS Race for Kids event.

Our snowmakers were hard at work last night, making snow on Hayride, Lower Hayride, North Slope, National & Easy Street. Today, we plan to continue making snow on North Slope, National, Hayride & Lower Hayride.

PLEASE NOTE-- ALL UPHILL TRAFFIC is prohibited on resort trails during operating hours. This is for the safety of our guests. Thank you.

For all the latest Stowe Mountain Resort updates please join us on Facebook, sign up for email alerts at Stowe.com or call 253-3000 to speak with an information specialist.

Caution

You may find unmarked obstacles such as rocks, ledges, stumps, trees, bare spots and variations in terrain. Do not proceed unless you accept responsibility for your safety and equipment, and are willing to confront all conditions and risks of a mountain environment. Lifts and trails exist in a mountain environment, and may close due to weather, usage and/or special maintenance.

Uphill Travel on Resort Trails is Prohibited during Operational hours. Stowe Mountain resort does not recommend the use of our trails during non-operational hours. Find out more here.

Backcountry Safety

Areas of Stowe Mountain Resort marked outside of the ski area boundary on trail maps and with signage on the mountain itself, is hazardous backcountry terrain, containing unmarked hazards such as cliffs, thick, brushy terrain, riverbeds, stumps, rocks & avalanches. This area is not patrolled or maintained. Vermont law states that any person who uses ski area facilities to access terrain that is outside the open and designated trails shall be liable for any costs of rescue, medical or other services.
