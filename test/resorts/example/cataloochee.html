Snow Report
Please remember to use GOOGLE MAPS to get your driving information to the area and NOT GPS. GPS will lead you onto incorrect roads that are impassable. DO NOT FOLLOW DIRECTIONS THAT CONTAIN THE WORDS HEMPHILL ROAD OR YELLOW PATCH ROAD. They are not correct.
On Tuesday, we will be skiing on 100% of the mountain on 17 slopes with three aerial lifts and two conveyor lifts operating on a base of 72-102" and a machine groomed surface. We have 4 features in Cat Cage and Upper Over Easy Terrain Parks.
On Tuesday, we will offer three sessions of skiing, day skiing from 9am-4:30pm, twilight skiing from 1pm until 10pm, and night skiing from 6pm until 10pm with a morning or afternoon half  day rate available. Remember Tuesdays are also Homeschool Days at Cataloochee and homeschoolers receive special discounts on lifts, lessons and rentals. And, don't forget every non-holiday Monday through Thursday all persons renting skis or snowboards at Cataloochee receive a FREE 1 1/2 hour beginner lesson! Lessons run throughout the day beginning at 9:30am.

Tube World located in Maggie Valley, across from Ghost Town in the Sky, four miles from the ski area will open from 4pm-10pm, with 1 hour 45 minute sessions available beginning at 4, 6, and 8pm. Cost for tubing is $25 and you must be 42" to ride alone. There is no two person tubing at Tube World. Wee Bowl, our children’s snowplay area is open and runs on the same session schedule as Tube World. Fee for Wee Bowl is $5 per session.
February continues Cataloochee's Appreciation Day series with Fire and Rescue Appreciation Days on Thursday, February 20th and Friday, February 21st. Fire and rescue personnel and their families receive significant discounts on lift tickets, lessons, rentals and tubing for day, twilight or night skiing. Check our website for more information.

Our rental shop in Maggie Valley, Cataloochee Ski & Sports, is open daily. Be sure to stop there for your discounted rentals and all accessories you might need prior to your day on the slopes. Don’t forget you can get your lift tickets and rental gear at both the shop in Maggie Valley and here at the area after 8pm for your next day’s visit, to save time on your arrival.
We remind you to be aware of changing ski conditions and use caution. Natural and manmade obstacles exist and snowmaking and grooming activities are routinely in progress. Please use control and ski or ride only on designated slopes and trails and please call this number anytime for current skiing conditions. Don't forget to join us on Facebook and Twitter and for our complete brochure right at your fingertips or to sign up for our e-newsletter Cat Tracks Online, look for us on the web at www.Cataloochee.com or visit www.kidsskifree.com for more information on our Kids Stay and Ski Free program. As always we do thank you for calling and look forward to seeing you on the slopes of Cataloochee this season!

March 2 is our BIG One Day Season Pass Sale and Seasonal Rental Order Day. Pass prices on this day will be the lowest of this year and next and you will be able to ski for the rest of this season and next on the pass you purchase on March 2. There will be special pricing not only on midweek passes but for every pass we offer. These special prices (including our midweek season pass for $199) will be available online beginning February 19 until sale day and then in person, over the phone or online on March 2. Pass pricing will go up on March 3. Also, seasonal ski and snowboard rentals will also be available for order now as well. This pass will not be valid for skiing until March 2. Click here for more information. Don’t forget if you have a current season pass to any other area, you can bring your pass to Cataloochee and receive $10 off your day’s lift ticket.

Google Maps provides the only accurate driving directions to the ski area from your point of origin. Google Maps is the ONLY mapping service to provide accurate directions at the current time. Please remember to use this service when planning your trip to the area. GPS devices and other mapping services provide directions over a limited access, unmaintained dirt road.
