Feb. 22, 2:15pm

Today's Update

Good afternoon, Sugarloafers. Between the red sky sunrise this morning and the winds coming in hot mid day, I'd say there's a storm on the way. Now, it won't be a blockbuster storm by any means, but the 1-3" of snow expected should act as a nice buffer to the trails. I checked out a mix of groomed and natural trails today, and everything continues to ski really great. It's so fun to have an open buffet when you head for the hill. We've settled into some fantastic mid-winter conditions, and thanks to the potential of a couple small bursts of snow this week, I think we'll close out February on a high note. 
Very light snow showers have started and the intensity should pick up a bit as we head into the evening hours. It's a quick moving system and should be wrapped up before midnight. For your Tuesday, you can expect a partly sunny sky and winds that have returned to a mild breeze. And temps are going to feel wonderful. The base will be between 30-34 degrees and summit between 22-26 degrees. 
The grooming squad has settled into their mid season groove, too. This is the time of the season when you can expect to see mostly the same trails groomed from night to night, with the occasional surprise thrown in. Tonight, Springboard is on their list, along with 64 other trails. Lift ops is back to the usual weekday schedule and you can anticipate to see usual 9 lifts scheduled for tomorrow.

A couple events to keep on your radar in the coming weeks - the Inferno race is back this year and it's changed up a bit from previous years. The XC ski cross event will take place on Sunday, March 14, and will start at the top of the Whiffletree lift and end of the Outdoor Center. The night before, the crew from the Maine Comedy All Stars will be performing in the King Pine Room, beginning at 7pm.      

Winter Safety Guidelines:

As a reminder, before coming to the mountain, please be prepared to do the following:

  • Prior-to-visit health screening: How are you feeling?
  • Face coverings are mandatory and required in all public areas, including lift lines and on the chairlift. 
  • Your car is your Base Lodge–that means you'll boot up at your car and should only bring with you what you can carry throughout the day.
  • Drive together, ride together.
  • For more details and a complete list of safety measures and operational changes, please visit sugarloaf.com/winter-operations.

   

Uphill Access Policy:

Please take a moment to kindly review our new Uphill Access Policy, updated January 8. Remember:

  • The mountain opens to uphill travel at 7:00am and to downhill traffic at 8:30am; please note that no downhill traffic is permitted prior to 8:30am. 
  • All uphill skiers and riders are required to have a valid season pass or day ticket; if you do not intend to ride a lift at any point during the day, you can purchase an Uphill Access day ticket for $10 from Guest Services.
  • For those skinning up Burnt Mountain, please be aware that we are conducting cat sking operations on the weekends and ask that should you hear a cat approaching, please move 6 to 8 feet away from the cat track for your safety.
 
   

Have a relaxful evening,

Sarah
