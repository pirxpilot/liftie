Surface Conditions
Our groomers are lined up and ready to harvest snow from our parking lots from the upcoming storm system. We're setting sights on at least a limited operation by the second weekend of December, but that is snow-dependent, and could change.
To accommodate the demand for this season, we've raised our base hourly rate range to $15 - $21+ an hour and have increased benefits for our team members. If you are passionate about the mountain and want to share it with others, apply for the many positions available on our jobs page.

General Comments
Up until the time we begin active set-up for winter operations i.e. track-packing/grooming, ramp building, chair seat installation, avalanche mitigation efforts, running rope/closures, tower-pads, etc. we will not enforce our restricted up-hill travel policy that is in place throughout the winter.
• There is currently no patrol or other assistance available.
• There is no hazard marking (low snow conditions create unexpected and barely visible hazards).
• Be aware of and stay clear of snow harvesting activities in the base area and parking lots.
• Visitors are on their own and we strongly encourage that they go elsewhere.
• As always, sledding is not allowed (there are many other areas on Mt. Hood that accommodate that activity).
Once we transition to set-up we will enforce our restricted up-hill travel policy (with the exception of our two designated routes for accessing the backcountry). During this period we will be actively working to get the hill ready for our guests. Once this begins, for everyone's safety (both our employees and guests), we ask that visitors respect our policy and go elsewhere for their turns.
